“The Daily 250”

Each day for 365 days, the programming has been laid out via the “Daily 250” (it’s actually MORE than 365 days :))

If possible, do the programming AS PRESCRIBED and to the BEST of your personal ability.

If you need to REGRESS a movement to suit your fitness level, go ahead and do so by following the progression chart that's listed within this guide below.

NONE of these Daily 250’s are to be done recklessly.

QUALITY form and technique are always at the top of the list.

If a Daily 250 is listed as “For Time”, you’re pushing the pace but NOT at the expense of poor form and technique so always error on the side of FORM and TECHNIQUE before "time".

As far as REGRESSIONS go...

Use the Progression listed when possible UNLESS it’s been regressed within the programming already.

For example... If I list:

“25 Strict HSPU” and you can’t do STRICT HSPU, regress to the next movement and do that UNLESS you can’t, do the next level regression from there.

Another example...

Let’s say you can do 5 Strict HSPU but that’s your absolute max. What I’d recommend here is to start off with doing 5 Strict HSPU, then regress down to the next level regression.

Just as long as you can maintain solid form and technique, that’s all that matters.

In addition, you can also choose to use Progressions and Regressions as a way to modify and change up some of the different "Daily 250" routines if you so choose.

This can actually be used as a great tactic to avoid excessive overlap with certain movements, which is FINE just as long as you’re consistent with recovery and mobility work J Breakdown
